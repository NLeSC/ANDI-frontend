0. Introduction. The ANDI website is a new platform for neuropsychological assessment. It was developed (and is currently owned and maintained) by the ANDI consortium on the basis of a research project sponsored by the Netherlands Organization for Scientific Research (NWO).

The ANDI website provides multi-test evaluation with modern multivariate statistical tools. It facilitates neuropsychological assessment with a web-based interface in order to support both qualified clinical practitioners and behavioral researchers.

The ANDI website maintains a large database of neuropsychological tests and provides a platform for online multi-test evaluation. As a user, you may upload (anonymous) patient data, i.e. a series of neuropsychological test scores and/or behavior ratings of either a group of patients or of a single patient. Subsequently, the ANDI website returns a statistical report that documents for each patient separately a detailed, multivariate analysis of the profile of test scores. The report indicates whether the patient deviates from the norm (e.g. whether he/she is cognitively impaired), and which of the series of tests are responsible for this deviation from the norm (e.g. in which cognitive domains he/she is impaired).

The report also gives point estimates with confidence intervals of the frequency with which the patientâ€™s score deviations occur in the normal population. The latter information is essential to decide whether there are dissociations of cognitive functions, a fundamental concept in clinical neuropsychology and cognitive brain research in general. Additionally, the report documents the size and the demographic characteristics of the sample to which the patient(s) was/were compared. The anonymous normative data will come from people who served as control subjects in numerous neuropsychological and other behavioral health investigations that have been conducted in the past decades. The (anonymous) patient data you uploaded is only used to generate the statistical report and is not stored by the ANDI website.
